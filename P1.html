Stephen Hawking - The Great Scientist, Physicist and Author.

Here's the timeline of Stephen Hawking

Stephen William Hawking was an English theoretical physicist, cosmologist, and author who was director of research at the Centre for Theoretical Cosmology at the University of Cambridge at the time of his death.

  • 1942 - Born on 8th January at Oxford, England.

  • 1950 - Hawking's father became head of the division of parasitology at the National Institute for Medical Research, the family moved to St Albans, Hertfordshire.

  • 1958 - with the help of the mathematics teacher Dikran Tahta, they built a computer from clock parts, an old telephone switchboard and other recycled components.

  • 1959 - Began his university education at University College, Oxford.

  • 1962 - Began his graduate work at Trinity Hall, Cambridge.

  • 1966 - His essay "Singularities and the Geometry of Space-Time" shared top honours with one by Penrose to win that year's prestigious Adams Prize.

  • 1971 - His essay titled "Black Holes" won the Gravity Research Foundation Award.

  • 1973 - Published his first book, The Large Scale Structure of Space-Time, written with George Ellis.

  • 1979 - He was elected as Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at the University of Cambridge.

  • 1993 - He co-edited a book on Euclidean quantum gravity with Gary Gibbons and published a collected edition of his own articles on black holes and the Big Bang.

  • 2001 - He published his book The Universe in a Nutshell.

  • 2009 - Hawking retired as Lucasian Professor of Mathematics.

  • 2017 - He was awarded an Honorary Doctorate from Imperial College London.

  • 2018 - He died at his home in Cambridge, England, on 14 March, at the age of 76.
